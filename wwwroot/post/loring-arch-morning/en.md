# Morning Inside the Loring Arch

![Morning Inside the Loring Arch](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Loring_Arch_Interior.jpg)

We visited the Loring arch before breakfast because the client kept saying she wanted the entry to feel quiet before it felt grand. The building gives a useful answer. It does not announce itself with ornament; it lets repetition do the work. Rib, shadow, rib, shadow, then a plane of light at the end of the span.

Back at the studio, we translated that rhythm into a forty-foot passage between mudroom and kitchen. The practical move is modest: three shallow beams, a continuous plaster reveal, and a bench that starts before the first doorway. The important part is that the arrival sequence now has a tempo instead of a hallway.

Arcus is a good host for this kind of note because its surfaces stay calm. Drawings, captions, tags, and source notes all have room to breathe, which makes the page feel like a studio log rather than a sales brochure.

Open items for the next review: reduce the first beam depth by two inches, test limewash under late afternoon light, and confirm whether the bench can hide the return-air grille without becoming fussy.
