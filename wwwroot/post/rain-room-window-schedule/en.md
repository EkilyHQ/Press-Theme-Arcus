# Window Schedule for a Rain Room

![Window Schedule for a Rain Room](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Rain_on_window.jpg)

The brief was simple: a room where rain is welcome but mud is contained. That usually becomes a mudroom, but the family wanted something less apologetic. They wanted a place to sit during storms, dry the dog, warm boots, and watch water move down the glass.

The window schedule now has three attitudes. The low awning window is for sound. The fixed corner glass is for watching the cedar hedge blur. The small operable unit near the bench is for clearing steam after everyone comes in at once.

We added a drain under the slate shelf after the second mockup. It is invisible in the rendering, but it changes the room from charming to useful. The shelf can hold wet gloves without turning the wall below into a maintenance problem.

What remains unresolved is the threshold. A rain room should not feel like a garage entry with better lighting. The next model will test a short plaster return so the room introduces itself before it exposes the utility wall.
