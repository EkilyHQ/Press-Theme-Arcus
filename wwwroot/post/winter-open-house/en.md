# Winter Open House Checklist

![Winter Open House Checklist](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Interior_of_the_Dining_Room_from_Lansdowne_House_MET_DT132.jpg)

Open houses expose every unfinished thought in a studio. Drawings that made sense on Tuesday suddenly need labels. Models need bases. Material samples need to stop looking like a pile of errands.

This year we arranged the room by question instead of by project. One table asks how a house receives guests. One asks how storage can become architecture. One asks what to do with north light when the view is not worth framing.

The checklist is ordinary and therefore useful: print one plan at half scale, replace the chipped plaster sample, write source notes for every public image, and move the coffee urn away from the pin-up wall.

A real studio site needs these small operational posts. They prove the practice has a calendar, a room, and people who have to tape drawings straight before visitors arrive.
