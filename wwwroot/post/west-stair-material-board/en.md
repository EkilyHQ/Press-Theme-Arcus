# Material Board for the West Stair

![Material Board for the West Stair](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/William_Morris_-_Trellis_wallpaper_design.jpg)

The west stair is only eight steps, but it is where the house changes mood. Downstairs is all shoes, raincoats, and school bags. Upstairs is quieter. The landing has to negotiate both conditions without becoming a decorative pause that nobody uses.

We pinned the wallpaper study beside three samples: white oak with a clear matte finish, a warm grey linoleum, and a handrail mockup rubbed with black wax. The trellis pattern is too dense for the final wall, but it helped us see the landing as a plane with movement rather than a leftover vertical surface.

The client asked whether the stair needed a color. The answer was yes, but not a paint-chip kind of color. It needed undertone: the green in the linoleum, the yellow in the oak, the almost-blue of the shadow under the rail.

Decision log: use the linoleum only on the landing, keep oak on the treads, and move the wall light six inches lower so the handrail casts a readable line at night.
